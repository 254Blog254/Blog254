Why Most Teachers Are Quietly Burning Out

By a Kenyan High School Teacher

Let’s be honest. Teaching in Kenya has always been demanding, but lately, it feels like we’re silently running on fumes...

1. The Workload is Relentless

Between lesson planning, endless marking, CBC records, discipline cases, club responsibilities, and sometimes even supervising boarding students — there’s barely room to breathe...

"Rest" has become a luxury. Teachers are constantly on, and there’s no pause button.

2. Emotional Labour is Underrated

We’re not just educators. We’re also counselors, mediators, mentors, and sometimes even stand-in parents...

3. Lack of Support Systems

There’s little to no psychological support for teachers...

4. Financial Stress Makes It Worse

Burnout isn’t just about emotional exhaustion. Financial pressure adds fuel to the fire...

5. The Stigma Around Speaking Up

There’s an unspoken rule: keep quiet and keep going...

So, What’s the Way Forward?

Schools need to normalize mental health conversations...

Final Thought

Burnout doesn’t always look like breaking down. Sometimes, it looks like a teacher staring blankly at her lesson plan…
